Project Overview

Although the benefits of regular exercise and muscle building on overall health and well-being are universally acknowledged, quantifying the intensity and effectiveness of a workout remains a challenge. Traditional metrics such as repetition counts, sets, and estimated loads provide a limited perspective on how much strain muscles endure during a session. Without a direct measure of muscular effort, it becomes difficult to gauge progress, optimize training, or prevent overexertion and injury.

Solution Approach

To address this, our project utilizes a single Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) to classify and count repetitions of upper body exercises, including bicep curls, and shoulder presses.

Muscle Analysis

In addition to tracking repetitions, the system analyzes the state of the muscle using EMG sensor data, allowing us to assess muscle fatigue during exercise.

Project Goals

This project is designed to predict the onset of muscular failure and help prevent overexertion and injury through continuous real-time monitoring.
